    Executive TL;DR
    ①

    Soft costs are now the dominant lever. Battery storage in the mid-market moved from policy-dependent to capex-positive in 2025. The structural shift is not LFP cell price decline (decelerating to 6.4% YoY) — it's the software-driven collapse of interconnection engineering, permitting, and commissioning costs.

    ②

    The bottleneck is utility interconnection, not capital. On 38 of 47 modelled sites, demand-charge avoidance pushed simple payback inside seven years. The barrier preventing faster growth is queue reform at utilities, not project economics or financing availability.

    ③

    DERMS software is the highest-margin, most contested layer. Operator software (DERMS) sits between the battery and market signals. Three competing architectures — vendor-locked, independent SaaS, and operator-built — each carry distinct margin and adaptability tradeoffs. This is the battleground layer for 2026–2028.

    Glossary

    DERMS
    Distributed Energy Resource Management System. Software layer between battery hardware and market dispatch signals.
    LFP
    Lithium Iron Phosphate. The dominant cell chemistry for mid-market stationary storage. More thermally stable than NMC.
    BTM / Behind-the-Meter
    Storage sited behind the utility meter at a commercial or industrial customer. Revenue from demand-charge avoidance and self-supply.
    FTM / Front-of-Meter
    Storage that transacts directly with the wholesale grid. Revenue from energy arbitrage, ancillary services, and capacity markets.
    IRR
    Internal Rate of Return. Unlevered unless specified. The synthetic portfolio median is 9.7%.
    Soft Costs
    Non-hardware project costs: interconnection engineering, permitting, commissioning, and financing. Now the dominant cost layer per the report.
    Mid-Market
    Projects between 250 kWh and 5 MWh of useable energy capacity. Sited behind a C&I meter or in front of a distribution feeder serving 50–5,000 customers.
    Interconnection Queue
    Utility review process for connecting new generation or storage to the distribution or transmission grid. Identified as the primary bottleneck for mid-market growth.
