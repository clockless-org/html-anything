    Pricing Page V2 — Decision Memo

    TL;DR — 3 key observations
    01
    The 4-tier pricing collapses to 2 published tiers (Starter + Team) plus a contact-sales Enterprise track — driven by Pro's failure to capture the intended prosumer segment.
    02
    Pro accounted for only 7% of Q1 signups but 31% of pricing-page time-on-page, and "pricing confusion" support tickets rose 2.4× year-over-year (412 vs 173).
    03
    Expected outcome: +18% activation rate, net-revenue-neutral by month 9, +6% baseline by Q4 2026 — with 1,184 existing Pro customers grandfathered during a 60-day migration window.

    Core claims
    Pro tier never found its market
    The "power individual" prosumer segment that Pro was designed for never materialised. Q1 2026 showed 71% Starter / 7% Pro / 22% Team. Pro creates cognitive load on the page without driving revenue.
    → §1 Context
    Simplification follows competitive consensus
    A synthetic competitor survey shows Linear (2 tiers), Vercel (2+E), Notion (3) — all ≤ 3 tiers. The four-tier model is an outlier, not a differentiator.
    → Appendix A
    Pro customers are protected but sunset-tracked
    All 1,184 Pro customers stay on their current rate in a grandfathered plan. A 60-day migration window with email outreach targets the 123 multi-seat accounts for upgrade to Team.
    → §2.2 Pro customers
    Short-term revenue dip, long-term gain
    The model projects −$210K ARR in months 1–6 (due to grandfathered Pro pricing), neutral by month 9 as Pro churns naturally, and +6% above baseline by Q4 2026 from higher Team conversion.
