html-anything

Turn files, folders, and URLs into self-contained interactive HTML pages — designed by an LLM for each source.

Not a format converter. The skill samples the source, finds the useful structure, designs the right UI for this content, then inlines the full data. Output is one self-contained .html file that works offline.

Use html-anything on ~/Downloads/_chat.txt
→ _chat.html  (1 file, opens in any browser)

✨Personal / experiential exports

🌍Google Maps — saved places →

Starred places.csv (34 places across 4 continents, synthetic) → personal world atlas

The skill walked the user through a Google Takeout export, then designed a scrollable map of every place they cared enough to save. Pins drop in on first load, click for the note they wrote at the time. The output is a story of intent — not a CSV.

🎧Spotify — listening history →

Streaming_History_Music_*.json (1,100 plays, 18 artists, 4 years, synthetic) → year-by-year scroll

The skill explained how to request the privacy export, then designed a "your life in Spotify" reveal-on-scroll page — top tracks per year, listening rhythm heatmap, count-up totals. Spotify Wrapped goes a year at a time; this is the lifetime view.

🟣Twitch — viewing history →

viewing_history.csv (480 sessions, 14 streamers, 3 years, synthetic) → top-streamers wall

Top streamers by hours, when-you-watched heatmap, category drift over time. Caedrel's name in display type. Fan shrine vibe — not a parental-control dashboard.

❤️Apple Health — your body, year by year →

export.xml (10K records + 245 workouts, 18 months, synthetic) → personal health story

Activity rings, sleep arc, workout calendar, resting-HR sparkline, hike-day outliers preserved. The skill is showing you your data, not diagnosing — Strava-style joy, no clinical voice.

📦Amazon — order history →

Retail.OrderHistory CSV (178 items across 3 years, 3 recipients, synthetic) → personal commerce memory

The skill walked the user through requesting their data, then designed a money-and-memory page: spend by year and month, "reorder DNA" panel with cadence inferred per item, category breakdown, recipient share, returns / refunds / cancellations callouts, and a searchable item drill-down. No product images, no Amazon CDN — completely offline.

▶️YouTube — watch history →

📖Kindle highlights →
