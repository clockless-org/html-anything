Sharon's routing note: 280N is consistently 6 min faster than 101N to San Francisco during peak — and more predictable. 85N at 5pm between Lawrence and De Anza is reliably bad (25–35 min for a 6-mile stretch). Buyers who commute to Palo Alto or beyond should factor that in; Monta Vista's northwest position saves 4–7 min vs. the southeast end of the city.
🍎
Apple Park commuters
Infinite Loop + Apple Park are both under 5 min from Garden Gate. If you're an Apple employee, this is the most economically rational neighborhood in Cupertino — school quality is high and you're not paying the MVHS premium you may not need.
🚂
Caltrain access
Mountain View Caltrain is the closest station — 12 min from central Cupertino by car. Sunnyvale station is 10 min. If you're SF-bound 3+ days/week, add $180–220/month for a Caltrain Zone 2 monthly pass to your housing cost model.
🔁
WFH correction
Post-2022 return-to-office mandates have brought peak-hour congestion back to ~85% of 2019 levels on De Anza and Stevens Creek. Sharon's buyers who planned on 2-day WFH flexibility should still verify live with their employer before using commute time as a tiebreaker.

Sharon's Three Takes

What Sharon Actually Tells Buyers

Not consensus. Not safe. The things Sharon says in the car between tours that you won't read in the neighborhood brochure.

1
If you want under $2.8M, look at 3-bed in Rancho Rinconada — not 4-bed anywhere else.
The 3-bed-in-RR vs. 4-bed-everywhere-else comparison almost always resolves to RR once you run the numbers. A 3-bed in Rancho Rinconada at $2.55–2.65M gets you the same school district as a 4-bed Garden Gate at $3.2M — and the 4th bedroom in GG is often a converted garage or a 9-foot box. Sharon's filter: if the 4th bed is under 100 sqft and has no closet, it's not a bedroom.
Buyer strategy
2
Monta Vista High is worth the $300K premium — IF your child is entering 7th grade or earlier.
The MVHS premium is real and durable because the demand for it isn't going away. But if you're buying with a 10th grader, you're paying for 2.5 years of high school access. Do the per-year math: $300K ÷ 2.5 years = $120K/year. For a 1st grader, that same $300K buys 12 years. The premium is a better investment the earlier you buy. Sharon will do this calculation with you before you submit anything.
School premium math
3
New construction in Cupertino is rare — 92% of inventory is 1960s–1990s. Budget $80–150K for cosmetic refresh on day one.
Don't let a seller's fresh paint and staged IKEA furniture absorb your renovation budget mentally. The bones are 40–60 years old: galvanized plumbing in anything pre-1980, single-pane in anything pre-1990, and R-13 insulation in most attics. Sharon's pre-offer walkthrough captures these items with photos. $80–90K gets you kitchen + primary bath. $130–150K gets you that plus windows and HVAC. Budget this before your offer, not after you've stretched the purchase price.
