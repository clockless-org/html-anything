Why this guide exists
Most first-time buyers start in the wrong order.

The typical Bay Area buyer finds a home on Zillow, falls in love with the kitchen, schedules a tour — and only then asks their bank what they can actually borrow. By the time they understand escrow timelines, contingency waivers, and why that $2.8M home listed $400K under comp isn't "a deal," they've already lost two offers and burned four weekends.

Sharon sees this pattern in almost every first-time buyer she meets. Not because they're careless — because no one hands them the right sequence up front. Lenders talk about rates. Listing agents talk about the market. Nobody maps the full 90-day path, start to keys.

This guide does that. It runs the same sequence Sharon walks every client through before their first tour: know your budget ceiling, get pre-approved, build your tour shortlist, submit a calibrated offer, survive escrow, and close on schedule. In that order. Nothing skipped.

The numbers in this guide are drawn from Sharon's closes in Cupertino, Palo Alto, Sunnyvale, Mountain View, and Los Altos — 2021 through 2024. Median prices, escrow lengths, and tour counts are real. School API scores, commute estimates, and neighborhood comps are sourced from CUSD and MLS data she uses in her own client briefings.

By the end, you won't be ready to win every offer. You'll be ready to compete — which is the actual job.

Chapter 07
The final week: walkthrough, wire, signing

Closing day is actually three separate events compacted into five days. The final walkthrough happens 24–48 hrs before signing to verify seller's repairs and confirm the home is in the agreed condition. Wire fraud is real — Sharon walks every buyer through phone-verification protocol before any funds move. Signing happens at the title company; recording happens at the county recorder; keys happen same-day or next morning.
